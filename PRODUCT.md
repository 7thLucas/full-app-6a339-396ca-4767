## Product Name
SmartCart AI — The AI-Powered Grocery Savings Platform ("Google Flights for Groceries")

## Users
- Budget-conscious households (families and individuals) who grocery-shop regularly
- Busy parents managing weekly meal planning and grocery runs
- Health-conscious shoppers tracking nutrition and dietary goals
- Cost-aware young adults establishing household routines

## Brand & Tone
Smart, practical, trustworthy. Empowers users to feel like savvy shoppers — not coupon-clippers. Confident, clear, frictionless. The product makes saving money on a recurring weekly spend feel effortless and intelligent.

## Core Problem
Consumers spend hours comparing grocery prices between stores. Most grocery apps show prices from only one retailer, making it impossible to find the best overall value without manual legwork.

## Core Solution (MVP scope)
An AI price-comparison and smart-checkout foundation that:
1. Lets the user create a grocery list by typing (photo/voice upload are stubbed/secondary entry points for now)
2. Compares prices across nearby participating stores and shows per-store totals
3. Recommends the single "best store" with ranked alternatives by price and distance
4. Lays the foundation for smart checkout (pickup/delivery)

## Phase 2 (Premium — do NOT block MVP)
- AI Meal Planner, Smart Recipe Generator, Pantry Management, Nutrition Assistant, Price Prediction Engine
- These are premium subscription features for a later phase.

## Business Model
Premium subscriptions (free core comparison + paid advanced features), delivery fees, grocery store partnerships, sponsored placements, digital coupons/cashback, white-label licensing.

## Strategic Principles
- Comparison is the product — the core value is saving real money on a recurring weekly spend.
- Data network effect — more stores and users make comparison more valuable.
- Help = income — users who save more than their subscription cost stay forever.

## Data Note
Use realistic seeded/mock store + price data where live grocery APIs aren't available. Make the mock data layer obvious in code so it can be swapped for real APIs later.

## Anti-references
- Not a coupon-clipping app. Not a single-retailer storefront. Not cluttered or gimmicky.
